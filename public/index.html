Trustworthiness

While it seems like trust in the web is at an all time low, it becomes more important than ever to understand the elements of a trustworthy and credible website.

Here are a few lessons I had learn while building my companies website. We must have in mind that not every user will know what to look for in a website in regards of safety. Most of the times they wont look for the lock placed on the left of the webadress that ensures they are using a secure connection with HTTPS, for example. They will trust their gut to tell them if said site is trustworthy or not. And we can design using that knowledge to our advantage.

Did you know that most costumers will automatically trust a website that displays a security seal? Be it from a security expert company such as McAfee or Norton, Google secure store, or the Paypal secure symbol. There are tests that you can run and earn those badges.

Most costumers wont "gut" trust a website if it looks wonky, badly designed, or is not delivering a clean user experience. They'll often look for external references and the authors/company bio before making a purchase. More often than not costumers will reject shopping websites that have external ads or are presenting irrelevant content when they perform a search action. Those are all signs of that gut reaction to the page.

SEO & PPC

SEO is the process of optmizing your website to rank in the organic results in search engines, free of charge.

PPC is an advertising model where you pay those search engines for clicks to your website. It is usually done on Google, but facebook marketing is also considered PPC, as you pay them for referals towards your website.

Do you remember wher our story started? We didn't have a website, and now we are thinking wether it is worth spending money to reach new clients or if we should focus on a more organic strategy, building content over time to rank our website on the first page of Google.

As stated in this search engine land article: There are two key differences when considering SEO or PPC. The first is that paid ads appear at the top of the page, above the organic listings influenced by SEO. The second is that traffic from organic via SEO is free, whereas traffic from PPC has a cost for each click. In many cases, SEO and PPC work best when integrated and strategically aligned.

Neither is better or worse than the other. Both are legitimate sources of traffic.
